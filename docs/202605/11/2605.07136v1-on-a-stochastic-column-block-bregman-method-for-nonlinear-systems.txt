# on a stochastic column-block bregman method for nonlinear systems 

Wendi Bao, Naiyu Jiang, Xing Lili, Li Weiguo 

College of Science, China University of Petroleum, Qingdao 266580, P.R. China 

Abstract —In this paper, we proposed a stochastic column-block nonlinear Bregman method with uniform probability to find the sparse solution of nonlinear systems of equations. Under certain assumption we conclude the upper bound for the convergence rates of this new method. Numerical experiments demonstrate that the efficiency of the new method. 

Index Terms —Nonlinear equations, Gradient descent method, Gauss-Seidel method, Block method, Bregman distance.

In [10] [11], Kaczmarz method was introduced into the Breg-man method obtain the sparse Kaczmarz method. Sch¨ opfer et al. [12] developed the randomized version of the sparse Kaczmarz (RSK) method and gave its convergence analysis. In [13], a weighted randomized sparse Kaczmarz (WRaSK) method was developed, which selects the i-th projection hyperplane with probability proportional to |⟨ ai, x k⟩ − bi|p,where 0 < p < ∞, for possible acceleration. Yuan et. al [14] used sketch-and-project to Bregman method and proposed the sketched Bregman projection (SBP) method. They generalized the concept of adaptive sampling to SBP methods, and showed how the single-step progress—measured by the Bregman dis-tance—depends directly on a sketched loss function. Tondji et al. [15] introduced a parallel (mini batch) version of RSK based on averaging several Kaczmarz steps and proposed Ran-domized sparse Kaczmarz with averaging (RSKA). Inspired of RSKA method Xiao et. al [16] introduced a flexible greedy selection strategy, which is adopted to adaptively choose the subset corresponding to the subvectors of the residuals with relatively large norms and developed the greedy average block sparse Kaczmarz (GABSK) method. Niu et. al [17] proposed a fast block sparse Kaczmarz (FBSK) method based on the Motzkin criterion and it converges linearly to the sparse solutions of the linear systems. While solving the sparse solution of nonlinear systems of equations is still in the developmental stage. By introducing the Bregman projection to nonlinear Kaczmarz (NK) method [4], Gower [18] proposed the nonlinear Bregman-Kaczmarz (NBK) method and relaxed nonlinear Bregman Kaczmarz (rNBK) method. To accelerate the speed of convergence, Xiao et al. proposed the averaging block nonlinear Bregman Kaczmarz method which is the row-block type of the NBK method. To accelerate the convergence of the NBK method, we consider sampling some columns of the gradient with uniform probability at each iteration. We assign a certain weight to the values computed for each column, sum these weighted values, multiply by the corresponding step size to perform the iteration, and propose the stochastic column-block nonlinear Bregman (SCBNB) method to find the sparse solution of nonlinear equations. The notations in this paper are expressed as follows. For any matrix A ∈ Rm×n, ∥ · ∥ , σmin , σmax denote the Euclidean norm, the maximum and minimum nonzero singular values of a matrix A, respectively. We perform some numerical experiments in Section III, which verify the efficiency of the SCBGD method and SCBNB method. Finally, we have the conclusions in Section IV.
