Abstract. Instruction-guided image editing requires balancing target modification with non-target preservation. Recently, flow-based mod-els have emerged as a strong and increasingly adopted backbone for instruction-guided image editing, thanks to their high fidelity and efficient deterministic ODE sampling. Building on this foundation, GRPO-based reward-driven post-training has been explored to directly optimize editing-specific rewards, improving instruction following and editing consistency. However, existing methods often suffer from noisy credit assignment: global exploration also perturbs non-target regions, inflating within-group reward variance and yielding noisy GRPO advantages. To address this, we propose RC-GRPO-Editing, a region-constrained GRPO post-training framework for flow-based image editing under deterministic ODE sam-pling. It suppresses background-induced nuisance variance to enable cleaner localized credit assignment, improving editing region instruction adherence while preserving non-target content. Concretely, we localize exploration via region-decoupled initial noise perturbations to reduce background-induced reward variance and stabilize GRPO advantages, and introduce an attention concentration reward that aligns cross-attention with the intended editing region throughout the rollout, reducing un-intended changes in non-target regions. Experiments on CompBench show consistent improvements in editing region instruction adherence and non-target preservation.

## 1 Introduction 

Instruction-guided image editing aims to modify a given image according to a target instruction, for example by adding, removing, or altering visual content and attributes, while preserving non-target content and the overall scene structure when required [4, 13, 27, 45]. Recently, flow-based models [1, 19, 21, 33, 39] have been increasingly adopted as a strong backbone for instruction-guided image editing [17], thanks to their strong realism and efficient deterministic ODE sampling.

In parallel, reward-driven post-training (e.g., GRPO-style optimization) has emerged as a practical way to improve instruction following and edit locality beyond supervised fine-tuning, by directly optimizing task-level rewards [22, 25, 31, 32, 34, 35, 38]. Nevertheless, extending GRPO-style post-training to these deterministic ODE editors remains non-trivial. To enable GRPO-style post-training for deterministic ODE samplers in flow-based [19], prior work injects exploration either by an ODE-to-SDE conversion that yields an equivalent SDE matching the original model’s marginal distribution at all timesteps [20, 33], or by a mixed ODE-SDE strategy with a sliding-window mechanism [18]. Alternatively, Neighbor GRPO [12] perturbs the initial noise to construct a neighborhood of candidate ODE trajectories and optimizes a softmax distance-based surrogate leaping policy, preserving deterministic ODE sampling and compatibility with high-order solvers [24]. However, in image editing, these exploration mechanisms exhibit a key mismatch: global initial noise perturbations also randomize non-target regions, inflating within-group reward variance and lowering the SNR of GRPO advantages [31, 34, 38]. This yields noisy credit assignment for the target edit and often manifests as a trade-off between editing region instruction adherence and non-target preservation (see Fig. 1). In this work, we propose RC-GRPO-Editing , a region-constrained GRPO post-training framework for flow-based image editing under deterministic ODE Region-Constrained GRPO for Flow-Based Editing 3

sampling, which improves localized credit assignment and strengthens editing region instruction adherence while maintaining non-target preservation. The framework couples region-constrained exploration with an attention-based intrin-sic reward to better align GRPO updates with the intended editing region. To reduce background-induced nuisance variance and improve localized credit assignment, we design Region-Decoupled Perturbation (RDP) to localize GRPO exploration to the intended editing region. Concretely, during post-training we construct a group of neighboring initial conditions by perturbing the initial noise only within the target region while suppressing perturbations elsewhere, so that candidate rollouts remain diverse where edits are expected but exhibit minimal unintended variation in non-target areas. To provide GRPO with a cleaner, locality-aware reward signal, we design Attention Concentration Density (ACD) as an intrinsic reward based on text-to-image cross-attention concentration along the rollout. Specifically, ACD quantifies the degree to which text-to-image cross-attention is concentrated within the intended editing region along the trajectory [5, 13]. This encourages policy updates to prioritize spatially aligned edits and reduces gradient noise from non-target regions. Overall, RC-GRPO-Editing couples region-constrained exploration with an attention-based intrinsic reward. Our contributions are summarized as follows: 

– We propose RC-GRPO-Editing, a region-constrained GRPO post-training framework for flow-based image editing under deterministic ODE sampling, which improves localized credit assignment and strengthens editing region instruction adherence while maintaining non-target preservation. 

– We introduce two components: RDP, which perturbs the initial noise only in the editing region to reduce background-induced variance; and ACD, an intrinsic reward that encourages cross-attention to stay in the editing region and provides an interpretable diagnostic. 

– Experiments and ablations show consistent improvements on foreground instruction adherence and background preservation, and validate the comple-mentary roles of RDP and ACD. 

## 2 Related Work 

2.1 Instruction-guided image editing 

A major line of work trains editors to follow natural language instructions, ranging from early models like InstructPix2Pix [4] and MagicBrush [42] to recent MLLM-driven frameworks like Step1X-Edit [23] and GoT [8] (see [46] for a survey). Despite strong instruction following, reliably confining changes to the intended region remains challenging [4, 13, 27]. A complementary direction performs editing with minimal training by control-ling the generative trajectory. Prompt-to-Prompt [13] and Null-text inversion [28] manipulate cross-attention or embeddings to steer edits. Other training-free or lightly supervised approaches manipulate latent spaces or diffusion trajectories for editing [2, 7, 16, 27, 29, 40]. Beyond text-only control, ControlNet [43] and FoI [11] 4 Z. Ouyang et al. 

leverage spatial conditioning and attention modulation to reduce unintended changes. These works highlight the central role of cross-attention for localized editing, motivating our attention-based signal. Alternatively, flow-based models (e.g., Flow Matching and Rectified Flow) learn continuous normalizing flows and straightened ODE transports for efficient deterministic sampling [1, 19, 21]. Our work builds on this deterministic ODE setting to stabilize reward-driven post-training for localized edits. 

2.2 Reward-driven post-training and GRPO-style optimization 

RL-based alignment improves instruction following and preference alignment for generative models. Representative methods include reward-driven RL fine-tuning (e.g., DDPO) and direct preference optimization variants [3, 36, 41]. Recently, GRPO [32] has been adapted to flow-based models: Flow-GRPO [20] and Mix-GRPO [18] inject exploration via equivalent or mixed SDEs, while Neighbor GRPO [12] perturbs initial noise to form deterministic ODE neighborhoods and optimizes a surrogate leaping policy. In editing settings, OmniRefiner [22] and Talk2Move [35] apply RL to localized refinement and geometric transformations. Unlike prior RL post-training that typically introduces global exploration, our work targets the image editing specific mismatch by constraining exploration to the editing region (RDP) and adding an attention-based reward (ACD) to mitigate cross-region coupling during deterministic ODE rollouts.

3.2 Motivation: Why Global Perturbation Hurts Credit Assignment 

In Neighbor GRPO, intra-group diversity arises solely from perturbations of the initial noise. For image editing, however, global perturbations randomize both the editing region and the background, introducing background-induced nuisance variance . ## 4 Methodology

4.2 Process Alignment via Attention Concentration Density

In practice, global attention interactions in Transformers can cause edit-related signals to propagate beyond the intended editing region, leading to excessive cross-attention spillover into non-target regions. This excessive spillover increases the residual cross-region term ∆interaction in Eq. (9) and can weaken localized credit assignment. We therefore introduce Attention Concentration Density (ACD) as a trajectory-level intrinsic reward to encourage region-focused attention over the entire rollout.

## 5 Experiments 

5.1 Experimental Settings Dataset. We evaluate on CompBench [15]. In our experiments, we use three task subsets provided by CompBench, namely Add , Remove , and Replace , with 982, 1,331, and 152 examples, respectively (2,465 examples in total, counted from the task metadata files). Each example includes a source image, an editing instruction, and an edit mask for separating the editing region from the non-target region. We follow the official CompBench evaluation scripts. 

Evaluation metrics. We adopt the official CompBench evaluation metrics. Fol-lowing CompBench conventions, we refer to the content inside the editing region 

M as the foreground and the content outside M as the background (non-target region). For non-target preservation, we report PSNR, SSIM [37], and LPIPS [44] computed on background regions only using M . For editing region instruction adherence, we report LC-I, defined as CLIP [30] image-embedding similarity between the edited foreground crop and the ground-truth (GT) foreground crop, and LC-T, defined as CLIP similarity between the edited foreground and the local textual description of the target attribute. 5.4 Discussion 

Our method improves localized credit assignment for deterministic ODE editing via region-constrained exploration (RDP) and a trajectory-level intrinsic signal (ACD). Despite these gains, two limitations remain. First, training requires an editing mask and access to cross-attention maps to compute ACD, which can limit compatibility with fused attention implementations that do not expose attention weights and may increase training overhead. Second, RDP enforces initialization-level edit locality at t=1 but does not guarantee trajectory-level locality throughout the rollout; cross-region couplings can still affect non-target regions, especially for edits requiring global consistency such as lighting and reflections. 

## 6 Conclusion 

We presented RC-GRPO-Editing, which resolves the credit assignment mismatch in flow-based image editing through region-decoupled perturbations (RDP) and attention concentration rewards (ACD). By suppressing background nuisance variance, our framework ensures localized updates that strictly balance instruction adherence with non-target preservation.
